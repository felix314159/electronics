# Goal

Rotary Button can select one of four LEDs (green, red, yellow, blue) to be active (only one LED can be active at the same time).
Pressing the rotary button does nothing (because we would be one attiny pin short).

# Parts

* Attiny85 (8 pins in total; Pin 1: PB5, Pin 2: PB3, Pin 3: PB4, Pin 4: Ground, Pin 5: PB0, Pin 6: PB1, Pin 7: PB2, Pin 8: VCC)
* sn74hc595 shift register (16 pins in total; Pin 1: Q_B, Pin 2: Q_C, Pin 3: Q_D, Pin 4: Q_E, Pin 5: Q_F, Pin 6: Q_G, Pin 7: Q_H, Pin 8: GND, Pin 9: QH', Pin 10: SRCLR, Pin 11: SRCLK, Pin 12: RCLK, Pin 13: OE, Pin 14: SER, Pin 15: Q_A, Pin 16: VCC)
* joyit COM KY 040 RE rotary encoder (5 pins: CLK, DT, SW, + (5V), GND)
* 4x led (green, red, yellow, blue)
* approx 5v power supply (e.g. 3x aa alkaline)
* 4x 330 ohm resistor for LEDs
* 2x 100 nF cap (attiny vcc/gnd, 74hc vcc/gnd)
* 1x 10 microF cap across power rails

# Wiring

## Attiny 

* Attiny Pin 2 with rotary encoder CLK
* Attiny Pin 3 with rotary encoder DT
* Attiny Pin 4 to breadboard gnd rail
* Attiny Pin 5 with 74hc pin 14
* Attiny Pin 6 with 74hc pin 11
* Attiny Pin 7 with 74hc pin 12
* Attiny Pin 8 to breadboard + rail
* Attiny Pin 8 has 100 nF cap to pin 4

## 74HC

* 74hc pin 10 to breadboard + rail
* 74hc pin 16 to breadboard + rail
* 74hc pin 16 has 100 nF cap to pin 8
* 74hc pin 8 to breadboard gnd rail
* 74hc pin 13 to breadboard gnd rail
* 74hc pin 15 through 330 Ohm resistor to LED Green (led cathode to breadboard gnd rail)
* 74hc pin 1 through 330 Ohm resistor to LED Yellow (led cathode to breadboard gnd rail)
* 74hc pin 2 through 330 Ohm resistor to LED Red    (led cathode to breadboard gnd rail)
* 74hc pin 3 through 330 Ohm resistor to LED Blue   (led cathode to breadboard gnd rail)

## Rotary encoder

* + to breadboard + rail
* gnd to breadboard gnd rail

## Power rail

* 10 microF cap between breadboard + rail (long leg at plus) and breadboard gnd rail

## Programming Notes 
We don't have external 10 k pullup so we probably should enable the ATtiny’s internal pullups in software for CLK/DT.
KY-040 encoders bounce a lot, so we will likely need software debouncing for rotation.
KY-040 boards usually already have pullups on CLK and DT, using INPUT_PULLUP usually still works fine.
