# Parts used

* 12 V DC fan, 2-wire
* Infineon IRLB4132PBF N-channel MOSFET
* 555 timer
* 10 kΩ potentiometer as the timing resistor
* 1 kΩ resistor
* 10 µF electrolytic capacitor
* 10 nF capacitor for pin 5 decoupling
* 100 Ω resistor in series with the MOSFET gate
* 100 kΩ resistor from MOSFET gate to ground
* 6× AA battery pack feeding a buck-boost converter, adjusted to 12 V output

# Wiring

## 555 wiring

* Pin 1:  GND
* Pin 8:  +12 V
* Pin 4 (RESET):  Pin 8 / +12 V
* Pin 5 (CONTROL):  10 nF capacitor:  GND
* Pin 2 tied directly to Pin 6 (timing node)

Timing network:
* 10 µF electrolytic capacitor
    * Positive leg:  timing node (pins 2+6)
    * Negative leg:  GND

* 1 kΩ resistor
* one side:  Pin 8 / +12 V
* other side:  Pin 7
* 10 kΩ potentiometer
* Wiper (middle leg):  timing node (pins 2+6)
* One outer leg:  Pin 7
* Other outer leg:  left unconnected

That makes the pot act as the adjustable resistor between pin 7 and the timing node.

## MOSFET and fan wiring

This is our low-side switch.

### IRLB4132PBF pinout

With the flat front facing you and legs downward:

left leg = Gate
middle leg and metal tab = Drain
right leg = Source


### Connections

Fan red wire:  +12 V
Fan black wire:  MOSFET Drain
MOSFET Source:  GND

#### Gate drive

555 pin 3 (output):  100 Ω resistor:  MOSFET Gate
MOSFET Gate:  100 kΩ resistor:  GND


As usually, all grounds in our circuit must be shared.

# What this circuit is doing

The 555 is configured as an astable oscillator.
The 10 µF capacitor charges and discharges between the 555 thresholds.
The 10 kΩ pot changes the timing.
Pin 3 outputs a switching waveform.
That waveform drives the IRLB4132PBF gate.
The MOSFET rapidly switches the fan’s ground path on and off.
The fan sees an average voltage/power level and changes speed.

The only problem is that 'low fan speed' is not really possible here.
You need certain speed to start the fan, and if u decrease too much it fully stops (basically u can go from full speed to still-pretty-fast, but no 'slow spin' mode possible here)
